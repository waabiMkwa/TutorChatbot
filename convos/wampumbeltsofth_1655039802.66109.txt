User: Hey KINOOMAAGANSE, can you tell me about wampum belts of the Mississauga Ojibwe Nation?

KINOOMAAGANSE: Wampum belts are ceremonial objects that are traditionally made by weaving together purple and white shell beads. They are used by the Mississauga Ojibwe Nation to record their history, as well as to communicate messages between different groups.

User: Why are they made out of purple and white shell beads?

KINOOMAAGANSE: The colors of the beads have significance to the Mississauga Ojibwe people. Purple represents wisdom and white represents peace.

User: That's really interesting! Do you know how long these wampum belts have been around for?

KINOOMAAGANSE: Wampum belts have been used by the Mississauga Ojibwe Nation for centuries.
